Tectonics and Erosion

What is the signature of tectonic uplift in the sedimentary records of basins adjacent to mountain ranges? I explored how uplift of a frontal tectonic “thrust sheet” (topographic barrier) (here modeled after the Iglesia Basin of Argentina) might influence erosion rates measured up and downstream of the uplifted thrust. I found that there is an interesting, transient signal characterized by an initial wave of low erosion rates, followed by high erosion rates. These signals broadly match observations within the Iglesia Basin.
