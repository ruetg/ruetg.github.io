Landscape evolution and dynamic topography

What can landscape evolution modeling tell us about the Earth’s mantle? Observations of mantle processes in the past are limited to near-surface observations, and therefore determining how the mantle interacts with the surface is critical if we want to evaluate geodynamic models. This coupled geodynamic-landscape evolution modeling work has shown how dynamic topography, the uplift or subsidence of the Earth’s surface induced by convection in the mantle, can influence the Earth’s surface and stratigraphic architecture.
